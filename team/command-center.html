MONEY ENGINE · how this actually pays

How we make money, and the levers we’d missed

Read this honestly: for the next 12 months NHD is not a media company that sells things. It is a trust engine whose P&L is measured in incremental Seider Realty buyer-side closings. Everything else is a test or a rounding error. Below is the real math, the levers ranked, and four money angles the plan hadn’t made explicit.

The only Year-1 math that matters

Year-1 revenue ≈ (incremental new-construction buyer closings attributable to NHD) × (avg buyer-side commission per new-build deal) + outbound referral fees.

You do not need thousands of readers or a viral tool. You need a modest, high-intent local audience and a single-digit-to-low-double-digit number of closings you can trace to the platform. That is the whole asymmetry that makes this viable: the content is customer acquisition for a transaction you already do, not a product that must monetize on its own. So the one number to instrument from day one is attribution: for every consult and every closing, log "how did this person find me?" If you can't point to NHD-sourced deals by day 90, the engine isn't running yet.

Four money levers we hadn’t made explicit

LEVER A · highest-intent, do now
"Get represented before you tour"

New construction is the one purchase where a buyer's agent is commonly builder-paid (no direct cost to the buyer), and most builder programs only credit the agent if they register / accompany the buyer on the first visit. Miss the first visit, lose the representation. That single rule is the cleanest editorial→representation bridge that exists. Turn it into an explicit path: "Touring this weekend? Have someone on your side before you walk in, it usually costs you nothing."

Caveat to state honestly on-site: post-NAR-settlement, builder co-op is now a patchwork, some pay fairly, some cut to flat fees, a few now push unrepresented buyers. Verify per builder. (That fragmentation is Lever D.)

LEVER B · fastest revenue, do now
Reactivate your existing sphere with the Dispatch

Your fastest dollars are not a new audience, they're your past clients and database, kept top-of-mind by a genuinely useful Friday email and warmed by real reporting. Path 6 (referral/past-client → NHD proof → representation) is the lowest-risk, highest-conversion path and will likely produce the first closing. The field-day call plan already does this; the Dispatch makes it recurring and scalable without more of your time.

LEVER C · defensible niche, build now
Be "the new-construction buyer's agent"

Most agents avoid or fumble new construction, the builder reps out-know them and they fear being cut out. NHD is proof you are the specialist. Two revenue effects: (1) buyers self-select to the person who obviously does this homework; (2) other agents refer you their new-build buyers they don't want to handle (inbound referral, or a referral fee). A niche personal brand backed by a public body of work is the durable version of "generate leads."

LEVER D · new data asset + money wedge
Track builder buyer-agent policy

Because co-op is now a per-builder patchwork, which builders still treat represented buyers fairly is (a) genuinely useful consumer intelligence nobody publishes, (b) a structured dataset that fits the database philosophy, and (c) directly relevant to your own deal economics. It's a field-collectable data point (you're already reading rate sheets). Editorial and revenue point the same direction: steer buyers, and yourself, toward the builders that play fair, warn them off the ones that don't.

GUARDRAILS · what protects the sprint

Stop-doing list + failure early-warnings

Stop immediately

  • Stop building new products, tools, calculators. Every new surface is a maintenance tax paid in the currency you have least of.
  • Stop expanding the Buyer Playbook, most copyable, least differentiated. Freeze as an SEO on-ramp.
  • Stop investing in Dot and native-app packaging. Low value; Dot is a trust liability. Park them.
  • Stop refining strategy docs. Further planning is procrastination against the 4 switch-flips. (This doc included.)
  • Stop treating web/mobile/app as 3 projects, it's one deploy + one optional packaging step.
  • Stop any motion toward market #2, licensing, franchise, employed reporters, partner-agent networks. Zero gates met.
  • Stop leaving the site un-deployed. The 401 is the most expensive thing on this list.
  • Stop entertaining ads / builder sponsorship / vendor kickbacks. Disqualified, not "later."
  • Builder-neutral compensation, forever: customary commission only, no builder-specific bonuses, spiffs, elevated co-op, "preferred builder" fees, or lender/title/insurance referral pay. If pay varied by builder, every recommendation would be compromised. (Decision record: strategy/compensation-independence-disclosure-standard.md)

Failure early-warnings (watch these)

  • Cadence breaks existential, a missed Friday, then two. → Cut surface, never quality. Most likely failure.
  • No demand despite launch existential, real traffic, zero consults @ 4wk. → Fix conversion, then question the market. Don't build more.
  • Operator overload existential, platform busy, closings zero. → Rebalance to conversations.
  • Trust compromised existential, a "lead-gen in disguise" accusation, or a tempting conflicted check. → Must be prevented, rarely cured.
  • Data goes stale manageable, aging "last verified" dates. → Slow coverage to protect accuracy.
  • Too many products manageable, rising maintenance load. → Freeze and retire.
Independence rule, on every page, forever: the reader must encounter the value several times before they encounter the offer once. Representation appears late and contextually, never early, never repeatedly. Every form states what happens next ("a real person replies within 24 hours; no automated sequence"). The conversion system's credibility is the product.
THE MACHINE · so nothing gets lost

Operating systems & weekly rhythm

Eight repeatable loops keep production going without reinventing anything. This is the index, the canonical runbook for each already exists in the project.

Weekly rhythm

  • Mon, Thu: field visits (≥1/wk) → structured rows first · database calls in outreach blocks · trade the mornings.
  • Fri 07:00 CT: Weekly Dispatch, Data Console (10 min) → template → One-Sheet + repurposed assets → gate → MailerLite → log Scoreboard row.
  • Per visit (48-hr closeout): post-visit flywheel, 1 field report → 1 Dispatch item → 1 social → profile updates.
  • Ongoing: reply to every form within 24 hrs (personally) · log attribution on every consult · weekly time-log check.

The 4 moats getting the effort (everything else is on-ramp)
  • 1 · The unbroken weekly time series, a year of dated Fridays no one can backfill. Costs only discipline. Highest priority.
  • 2 · The rate-sheet / MUD-PID document archive, collected on-site, indexed by nobody. Free byproduct of field visits.
  • 3 · The corrections + honest-uncertainty record, structurally impossible for advertiser-funded competitors to copy. Costs only nerve.
  • 4 · Named-source relationships + revisit deltas, "model count 6→4, two reps gone." Operator-time-intensive; pace it.

Not moats (don't confuse them): the website, tools, calculators, PWA, Playbook, Dot, "brand," or the "operating system" framing. All copyable in weeks. They are the delivery surface, not the defense.

Expansion gates, the minimum before a second market is even a question

Staying Central Texas and doing it extremely well is a good outcome, not a failure. Do not enter a second market until ALL hold: 26+ consecutive Dispatches · a field process a second person could run · demonstrated repeat traffic + subscriber retention · steady qualified consults · a proven consult→rep rate · closings traced to the platform · known unit economics · verified (not just researched) home-market saturation · a documented conversion funnel you can state in numbers. Then choose the market by observed relocation demand, not by which city sounds exciting. Most realistic true-expansion model: Pete/Seider operate one adjacent Texas metro directly (preserves quality control + one brokerage footprint). Reject franchise/licensing near-term.
